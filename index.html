Research Interests

During my masterâ€™s and PhD studies, I focused on developing efficient machine learning algorithms under limited supervision, particularly in areas such as noisy-label learning, partial-label learning, and (partial) multi-label learning.

Motivated by the rapid advancement of large (vision-) language models, my research has shifted toward building safe and reliable foundation models, e.g., uncovering and defending against backdoor and jailbreak attacks on vision-language models.

Currently, I am also intersted in exploring reinforcement learning techniques to train LLM-based agents for real-world applications, such as [verl-agent]. My long-term goal is to develop efficient agents capable of robust decision-making in real-world tasks.
